Palabok (Seafood and Pork)

Pancit Palabok: Filipino Noodles with pork & shrimp

a classic Filipino noodle dish featuring thin rice noodles generously coated in a savory, bright orange shrimp and pork sauceIt is heavily garnished with an assortment of toppings, including crushed chicharron, smoked fish flakes, hard-boiled eggs, shrimp, and fresh scallions.

Ingredients

  • 250g Palabok noodles
  • 150g Ground pork
  • Tinapa flakes
  • Shrimp bouillon cube
  • Annatto powder
  • All-purpose flour or cornstarch
  • 2 cups of Water
  • Fish sauce
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Black pepper
  • Cooking oil
  • chicharron
  • Sliced boiled egg
  • Sliced String Onion

Steps

  1. Place 200g–250g of palabok noodles in hot water for 15–20 minutes until softened, then drain.
  2. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Cook the soaked noodles for 3 to 5 minutes until tender but firm (al dente). Drain well, transfer to a serving platter, and cover so they do not dry out.
  3. In 2½ cups of water, boil the shrimp for 2 minutes until pink. Remove the shrimp, peel them, and set aside. Save the boiling water—this is your shrimp stock!
  4. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over low heat. Fry minced garlic until golden brown and crispy. Scoop out the garlic bits and set aside, leaving the oil in the pan.
  5. In the same garlic-infused oil, sauté the onions and minced garlic over medium heat until fragrant.
  6. Add the ground pork. Cook for about 5 minutes until it turns light brown.
  7. Stir in 2 tablespoons of tinapa (smoked fish) flakes and the fish sauce. Cook for 1 minute.
  8. Pour in the 2½ cups of saved shrimp stock and drop in the shrimp bouillon cube. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 5 minutes.
  9. Pour in the diluted annatto (atsuete) water. Stir well until the sauce turns a bright golden orange.
  10. Turn the heat to low. Whisk in your flour or cornstarch slurry (thickener mixed with a bit of cold water) while stirring constantly. Simmer for 2 to 3 minutes until the sauce becomes a thick, glossy gravy. Season with pepper to taste.
  11. Pour the thick, hot orange sauce generously over the cooked noodles on your platter.
  12. Arrange the sliced hard-boiled eggs and boiled shrimp on top.
  13. Sprinkle the crushed chicharron, toasted garlic bits, and chopped green onions evenly across the dish.
  14. Serve immediately with calamansi or lemon wedges on the side to squeeze over the noodles right before eating.
