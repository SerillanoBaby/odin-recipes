Pork Sinigang: Filipino sour soup featuring pork ribs

Sinigang na Baboy is a classic Filipino sour soup with tender pork belly or ribs simmered in a vibrant, tangy broth made from tamarind. It is packed with local vegetables like radish, eggplant, long beans, and leafy greens, making it a comforting and deeply satisfying dish traditionally served over warm white rice.

Ingredients

  • 500g Pork belly (liempo)
  • Onion
  • 2 Tomatoes
  • 4-5 cups of Water
  • Tamarind Soup base mix (Sinigang Mix)
  • 2 tbsp Fish Sauce
  • White Radish
  • Small eggplant
  • String beans
  • Long green chilli peppers
  • Water Spinach
  • Ladyfinger

Steps

  1. In a large cooking pot, combine the pork, onions, and tomatoes.
  2. Pour in the water (or rice washing) and bring to a boil over high heat.
  3. Once boiling, lower the heat to medium-low, cover the pot, and simmer for 30 to 45 minutes until the pork is tender. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface.
  4. Pour in the fish sauce and the tamarind soup base mix.
  5. Stir well and let it simmer for 2 minutes to let the pork absorb the tangy flavor.
  6. Add the sliced radish and long green chili peppers first. Cover and cook for 3 minutes.
  7. Add the string beans and eggplant. Simmer for another 3 to 4 minutes until all vegetables are fork-tender.
  8. Turn off the heat and stir in the kang kong or bok choy leaves.
  9. Cover the pot and let the residual heat wilt the leafy greens for 1 minute.
  10. Transfer to a large serving bowl and serve steaming hot with white rice.
