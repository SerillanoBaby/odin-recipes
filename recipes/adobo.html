Adobo (Chicken)

Chicken adobo: is the unofficial national dish of the Philippines.

It features chicken braised in a savory, tangy, and slightly sweet sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and whole black peppercorns. As it simmers, the ingredients reduce into a rich, flavorful glaze that pairs perfectly with steamed white rice.

Ingredients

  • 500g - 600g Chicken
  • Soy Sauce
  • White Vinegar
  • Garlic
  • 3 Dried Bay leaves
  • Brown Sugar
  • Whole Black Pepper
  • Cooking oil
  • Half cup of Water

Steps

  1. Combine the chicken pieces, soy sauce, and crushed garlic in a pot. Let it boil for 15mins-20mins.
  2. Heat cooking oil in a large pan or pot over medium-high heat.
  3. Remove the chicken, ensuring you save the remaining liquid for later.
  4. Sear the chicken pieces for 2 to 3 minutes on each side until browned. Pour the reserved boiled liquid
  5. Add the water, whole black peppercorns, and dried bay leaves.
  6. Bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat to medium-low.
  7. Cover the pan and simmer for 20 to 25 minutes until the chicken is completely tender.
  8. Pour in the vinegar and brown sugar.
  9. Leave the lid off and let it boil for 3 to 5 minutes without stirring. This allows the harsh, raw alcohol taste of the vinegar to cook off naturally.
  10. Stir gently and let the sauce simmer for another 5 minutes until it reduces into a slightly thick, glossy glaze that coats the chicken.
